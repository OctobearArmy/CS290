Great White Sharks

When most people think of sharks the image that comes to mind is the great white shark. Hollywood is part of the reason for this, Jaws itself was modeled after a great white shark. While the fear may have some truth behind it, since great whites are responsible for the largest number of fatal shark attacks globally, the occurrence is still incredibly rare, with numbers coming in at less than ten around the world annually. These fish are the top dogs of the ocean in most cases, although they do fall victim to packs of hungry Orcas who enjoy the liver of a great white as a fatty snack. Great whites can be found in the surface waters of all oceans, temperate and tropical, and they usually hang out near the coast because their favorite snack, seals, can be found there. They migrate often to breed or to feed, and because of this combined with their size, keeping a great white in an aquarium is not a feasible idea even though it has been tried in the past. These sharks are huge! The average males grow to be 12 feet on average, while the females generally closer to the 15 or 16 foot mark. The largest recorded great white was a female who measured just over 19 feet and tipped the scales at over 4,000 lbs! Although these are some of the biggest sharks known to man, they are not invulnerable. In fact, great white sharks are classified as vulnerable in population globally, and critically endangered in Europe. Some countries like Australia have specific conservation laws written to help the survival and rebuilding of the great white shark population.
